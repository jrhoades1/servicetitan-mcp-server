### Move 3: Help Neill reach his full potential ($170K)

Neill is a talented tech with room to grow. When he charges the house rate, takes every job, and works a full day, he goes from **$95K to $135K in earnings**. That's a $40K raise he gives himself.

**How to get there (mentoring, not managing):**
- **Pair him with Dan or Tom for ride-alongs** — let him see how they handle the jobs he's been discounting or walking away from. No lectures, just show him what a $600 ticket looks like vs his $510
- **Weekly ticket review** — 10 minutes, look at his completed jobs vs house avg. "Hey Neill, you left $43/job on the table this week — that's $600 in your pocket you missed." Make it about HIS money
- **Tie the quarterly bonus to 3.5 jobs/day** — most of the team already qualifies. This just makes sure everyone's pulling a full day. Neill earns more, not less

**The conversation:** "Neill, you're leaving $40K a year on the table. I want to help you get it."

**That's not pushing — that's investing in your guy.**

### Move 4: Alan returns + Tom/Kris bridge the gap ($100K)

Alan when healthy was the **highest-volume house tech** — 17.9 jobs/week, more than Dan or Tom. While he's recovering and Collin is ramping:

- Route 2-3 extra jobs/week to Tom (most revenue per hour on the team)
- Route 2-3 extra to Kris (solid and consistent)
- Both earn $10-14K more from the extra volume
- **This bridges the gap until Collin and Alan are both at full capacity**
