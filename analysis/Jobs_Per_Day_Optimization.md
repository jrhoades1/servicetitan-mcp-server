## Levers to Optimize Jobs/Day

1. **Dispatch efficiency** — tighter routing, less windshield time between jobs
2. **Reduce no-shows/cancellations** — confirmation calls, overbooking slots
3. **Shorten avg job duration** — better pre-screening so techs arrive prepared
4. **Extend capacity** — earlier start times, staggered schedules
5. **Reduce callbacks** — first-time fix rate means fewer repeat visits eating slots

---

## The $3M Threshold

Currently at **$2.75M**. Adding just **+2 jobs/day across the whole team** reaches **$2.92M** — approaching **$3M without adding a single tech or truck**.

## Case Study: Neill — The Quarterly Bonus as Early Checkout Enabler

### The Problem

Neill works **~24 hrs/week** including portal-to-portal drive time (~2.7 jobs/day) and earns **$94,656/yr** — an effective rate of **~$75/hr**. He's not short on dispatches. He goes home early because the comp structure lets him.

> *ServiceTitan only captures on-site clock time (~19 hrs/wk for Neill). Actual paid time includes ~1 hr/day drive time home-to-home.*

### How the 3% Quarterly Bonus Enables This

| | Without Bonus | With $3K/Qtr Bonus |
|--|--:|--:|
| Needed from commission/yr | $94,656 | $82,656 |
| Jobs/week needed at $550 × 21% | ~15.7 | ~13.7 |
| Jobs/day (5-day week) | ~3.1 | **~2.7** |

The ~$12K/year in quarterly bonus lets Neill **skip ~2 jobs per week** and still land at $95K. Those are exactly the 2 jobs/week this analysis identifies as missing revenue.

### What Neill Leaves on the Table

| Scenario | Jobs/Day | Hrs/Wk | Annual Comp |
|----------|--------:|-------:|-----------:|
| Current (goes home early) | ~2.7 | 19.3 | $94,656 |
| Picks up 1 more/day | ~3.7 | ~25 | ~$120,000 |
| Matches team avg | ~3.7 | ~25 | ~$120,000 |

Neill leaves **~$25K/year** on the table — costing the business **~$100K in revenue** — because the bonus structure lets him coast at 2.7 jobs/day.

### The Structural Issue

The 3% quarterly bonus is **not tied to volume or utilization**. For high-output techs like Dan, it rewards effort. For Neill, it's a **floor that subsidizes early checkout**.

## Bonus Restructuring Options

### Option 1: Minimum Job Count to Qualify

Quarterly bonus requires averaging ≥ 3.5 jobs/day (or ≥ 17.5 jobs/week).

| Tech | Current Jobs/Day (est.) | Qualifies? | Impact |
|------|------------------------:|:----------:|--------|
| Dan | ~4.2 | Yes | No change |
| Tom | ~3.8 | Yes | No change |
| Danny | ~4.2 | Yes | No change |
| Kris | ~3.5 | Borderline | Slight push |
| Jesse | ~3.6 | Yes | No change |
| Neill | ~2.7 | **No** | Must add ~1 job/day |
| Kaleb | ~3.4 | Borderline | Slight push |
| Alan | ~2.5 | **No** | Must add ~1 job/day |

### Option 2: Graduated Bonus (Tiered by Volume)

| Avg Jobs/Day | Bonus % | Quarterly Payout (est.) |
|-------------:|--------:|------------------------:|
| < 3.0 | 0% | $0 |
| 3.0 – 3.4 | 1% | ~$1,000 |
| 3.5 – 3.9 | 2% | ~$2,000 |
| 4.0+ | 3% | ~$3,000 |

*This rewards the behavior you want without punishing anyone — just makes them earn it.*

### Option 3: Volume Kicker (Replace Flat Bonus)

Eliminate the flat 3% quarterly bonus. Replace with:
- **21% commission** on jobs 1–3 per day (no change)
- **24% commission** on job 4+ per day

| Tech | Jobs at 21% | Jobs at 24% | Impact vs Current |
|------|------------:|------------:|------------------:|
| Dan (4.2/day) | 3 | 1.2 | Earns slightly more |
| Neill (2.7/day) | 2.7 | 0 | Loses bonus, must work more to compensate |
| Team avg (3.7/day) | 3 | 0.7 | About neutral |

### Option 4: Daily Dispatch Priority

Not a pay change — an operational rule:
- Techs who went home early yesterday go to the **back of the dispatch queue** today
- Consistent full-day techs get **first pick** of premium jobs

*Costs nothing. Creates a natural consequence for checking out early.*

---

## Recommendation

**Option 2 (Graduated Bonus)** is the cleanest:
- No one loses earning potential — they just have to show up
- Doesn't require changing commission rates (avoids renegotiation)
- Easy to explain: "The bonus still exists, it's just tied to effort now"
- Directly addresses the Neill problem without singling him out
- Most techs already qualify at current output — minimal disruption

**Combined with Option 4 (Dispatch Priority)** for a zero-cost behavioral nudge.
