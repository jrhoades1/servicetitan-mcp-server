## House Division — Per-Tech Analysis

### 1. Neill — VOLUME + EFFECTIVENESS (~$170K Opportunity)

**Three problems, not one:**

Neill was injured in Q2 (45 jobs). In healthy quarters he averages 200 jobs/quarter (15.3 jobs/week). That's mid-pack — not the worst, but well below Tom (17.7) and Alan when healthy (17.9).

The bigger issues: he **discounts jobs** and **turns customers away**.

**Discounting:**

| Quarter (Healthy) | Neill Avg Ticket | House Avg | Gap |
|--------------------|---------------:|---------:|----:|
| Q1 | $492 | $543 | -$51 |
| Q3 | $518 | $557 | -$39 |
| Q4 | $520 | $555 | -$35 |
| **Weighted Avg** | **$510** | **$553** | **-$43** |

At 15.3 jobs/week, $43/job = **~$34K/year left on the table from discounting.**

**Turning away work:**

Neill tells customers "we can't help you" — those jobs go to zero. If he turns away even 2/week: 2 x $553 x 52 = **~$57K/year in lost revenue.** Plus the customer never calls back.

**Going home early:**

15.3 jobs/week vs Tom's 17.7 = 2.4 fewer jobs/week x $553 = **~$69K/year.**

| Issue | Revenue Impact |
|-------|---------------:|
| Discounting ($43/job below house avg) | -$34K/year |
| Turning away work (est. 2/week) | -$57K/year |
| Going home early (2.4 fewer jobs/wk vs Tom) | -$69K/year |
| **Total Neill gap** | **~$170K/year** |

**The Fix:** This is not just an incentive problem — it's coaching + accountability.
1. Ride-alongs to address discounting and "can't help" calls
2. Tie quarterly bonus to minimum job count (3.5/day)
3. Review his completed jobs vs team avg ticket monthly

**What Neill gets:** If he matches house avg ticket and volume, he'd earn ~$135K (up from ~$95K). He'd be making $40K more per year.
