## What We're Asking

Tracy's compensation should reflect both her role and the value she brings. A few options to consider:

**Option A — Catch-Up Raise + Annual Tie-In**
Apply the same ~6% pricing increase to Tracy's base: $89,000 to $94,340. Going forward, adjust her base annually by the same percentage as average ticket growth. This keeps her from falling behind again.

**Option B — Hourly Rate Parity**
Matching the average tech's effective hourly rate of $60.21 at Tracy's 42 hrs/week = ~$131,500/year. Even at 80% parity (recognizing different role types) that's ~$105,000/year.

**Option C — Base + Revenue-Tied Bonus**
Raise the base and add a quarterly bonus tied to team revenue — similar to what the techs already get. For example, a bump to $95K base + 0.5% of quarterly revenue would put Tracy around $108K total, and her pay would grow naturally as the business grows. The exact split between base and bonus is flexible — the key is that Tracy benefits when prices and revenue go up, just like everyone else does.

---

The principle is straightforward: when the business raises prices and revenue grows, everyone who contributes to that revenue should benefit — not just the field team.
